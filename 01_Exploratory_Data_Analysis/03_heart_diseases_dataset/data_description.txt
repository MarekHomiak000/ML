Age — Patient’s age in years.

Sex — Biological sex (often 0 = female, 1 = male).

ChestPainType — Type of chest pain (e.g., typical angina, atypical, non-anginal, asymptomatic).
                3: TA (Typical Angina) = biggest chest pain
                2: ATA (Atypical Angina)
                1: NAP (Non-Anginal Pain)
                0: ASY (Asymptomatic)

RestingBP — Resting blood pressure measured in mmHg.

Cholesterol — Serum cholesterol level in mg/dL.

FastingBS — Fasting blood sugar > 120 mg/dL (1 = yes, 0 = no).

RestingECG — Resting electrocardiogram result.
             0 (Normal): Used as the baseline, healthy, or lowest-risk state.
             1 (ST): Represents a moderate concern
             2 (LVH): Represents a significant structural change

MaxHR — Maximum heart rate achieved during exercise.

ExerciseAngina — Exercise-induced angina (1 = yes, 0 = no).

Oldpeak — ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest.

ST_Slope — Slope of the ST segment during peak exercise.
            0: Low Risk (Up)
            1: Moderate Risk (Flat)
            2: Highest Risk (Down)

HeartDisease — Target variable (1 = disease present, 0 = no disease).
